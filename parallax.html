CHILD LABOR


TITLE

Child labor is defined as the use of children in industry or business, especially when illegal or considered inhumane. Much of the work performed by children working in agriculture is highly risky and hazardous. The main causes of child labor in agriculture come from poverty, lack of education, and traditional values. 60 percent of all child labor worldwide, from ages 5 through 17, work in agriculture. This value equates to about 98 million children.
